Chameleons or chamaeleons (family Chamaeleonidae) are a distinctive and highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202 species described as of June 2015.

These species come in a range of colors, and many species have the ability to change color. ... Chameleons are adapted for climbing and visual hunting.

Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Their hands and feet have large toes that help them cling to branches.
